Property Management Rights — Complete Reference
▶1. What is PMR?

PMR (Property Management Rights) is a uniquely Australian business model, most common in Queensland. It combines two roles:

  • Caretaking — maintaining the common areas of a residential complex (gardens, pools, BBQ, laundry)
  • Letting — managing short-term or long-term rentals for unit owners

How It Works

The Body Corporate (strata committee) appoints a manager via a Management Rights Agreement. The manager receives:

  • BC Salary — fixed annual payment from the Body Corporate for caretaking duties
  • Letting Commission — percentage of rental income when managing units
💡 Think of it as a turnkey business: buy the agreement, move in (optional), run the operations.

Three Types of PMR

TypeWhat You BuyTypical Price
Business OnlyCaretaking + Letting agreement only$500K - $1.5M
Business + Residential (MLU)PMR business + manager's apartment$1M - $3M+
Business + FreeholdPMR business + own a unit in the complex$2M - $5M+

Key Distinction: Permanent vs Holiday

  • Permanent — long-term rentals (6-12 months), stable income, lower returns
  • Holiday — short-term bookings (Airbnb-style), higher returns, more work
  • Mixed — combination of both (most common in Gold Coast)

▶3. How to Evaluate a PMR Deal

The 6-Point Checklist

① BC Salary % of Net Income

This is THE most important metric. It shows what % of your income comes from fixed salary vs letting business.

BC%MeaningAction
50-70%Healthy balance✅ Good PMR investment
70-80%Leaning caretaker⚠️ Acceptable with caution
80%+Caretaker trap❌ Avoid — no real letting business
BC% = BC Salary / Net Income × 100

② Multiplier (Price / Net Income)

Multiplier = Asking Price / Annual Net Profit
MultiplierMeaning
3-5xExcellent value
5-6xFair market value
6x+Overpriced
💡 A 4x multiplier means you recoup your investment in 4 years.

③ Letting Pool Size & Ratio

Total units in the complex and how many are in the letting pool.

Pool Ratio = Pool Units / Total Units × 100
✅ Higher ratio = more letting income potential. Ideal: 30-60%
🚨 Below 15% = minimal letting income; Above 70% = high management workload

④ Per-Unit Weekly Commission

$/wk = (Net Income - BC Salary) / Pool Units / 52
$/weekMeaning
$60-85Normal Brisbane range
$40-60Below average
<$40Abnormally low — investigate!
🚨 If per-unit commission is under $40/week, the letting business may be unprofitable or the BC salary inflated.

⑤ Contract Years Remaining

YearsMeaning
15+ yearsExcellent security
10-15 yearsGood — verify top-up history
<10 yearsRisk — needs top-up strategy
💡 "Top-up" means extending the agreement via Body Corporate vote. A history of successful top-ups signals a supportive BC.

⑥ Operational Burden

  • On-site required? — Must you live in the complex?
  • Office hours? — Fixed reception hours vs flexible contactable
  • Facilities — Pool, gym, BBQ, lift? More facilities = more caretaking work
  • Sub-contractors — Can you outsource gardening/cleaning? (Ideal)

▶5. Legal & Regulatory

Governing Law

Queensland: Body Corporate and Community Management Act 1997 (BCCM Act)

This sets rules for:

  • How management agreements are structured
  • What rights the manager has
  • How top-ups and renewals work
  • Dispute resolution processes

Licensing Requirements

  • Caretaking only — No license needed
  • Letting + caretaking — Resident Letting Agent (RLA) license required
  • Property management — Full real estate license if managing off-complex properties

Agreement Modules

ModuleTypical UseTerm
Standard ModulePermanent residential complexesUp to 25 years
Accommodation ModuleHoliday/tourist complexesUp to 25 years
Commercial ModuleMixed-use buildingsVaries

Due Diligence Checklist

  • ☐ Full management agreement (all terms)
  • ☐ 3 years financial statements (audited)
  • ☐ Body Corporate meeting minutes (last 3 years)
  • ☐ Current letting pool schedule
  • ☐ Top-up history and next due date
  • ☐ Maintenance backlog report
  • ☐ Insurance certificate
  • ☐ Competing management rights nearby
  • ☐ Occupancy rates and trends
▶6. Market Overview — Brisbane & Gold Coast

Brisbane Market

  • ~600+ management rights complexes
  • Strong demand due to 2032 Olympics
  • Typical multiplier: 5-7x for permanent
  • Focus: Suburbs within 10km of CBD
  • Average net yield: 15-20%

Gold Coast Market

  • ~1,200+ complexes (largest in Australia)
  • Holiday/short-term dominant
  • Higher volatility but higher returns
  • Key suburbs: Surfers Paradise, Broadbeach, Burleigh, Mermaid Beach, Main Beach
  • Average net yield: 18-25%

Market Trends (2026)

  • Interest rates stabilizing at ~5.8-6.2%
  • Olympics infrastructure driving Brisbane growth
  • Tourism recovery boosting Gold Coast holiday lets
  • Airbnb/short-term regulation tightening
  • Management rights prices up ~8% year-on-year

▶8. Common Pitfalls & Red Flags
🚨 BC Salary >80% of net income = Caretaker trap
You're a janitor with no real letting business. No growth potential.
🚨 Contract <8 years remaining
Top-up may fail. If BC votes "no," your income drops to $0.
🚨 Negative or weak Body Corporate relationship
Check meeting minutes for disputes. A hostile BC = future nightmare.
🚨 High maintenance backlog
If the complex needs $500K in repairs, YOUR caretaking workload doubles.
🚨 Owner-occupier majority (>60%)
Owner-occupiers hate short-term lets and often vote against letting pool expansion.
🚨 Competing management rights next door
Another manager across the street can steal your letting pool.

Questions to ALWAYS Ask

  • How many successful top-ups in the last 10 years?
  • What's the current occupancy rate?
  • Are there any maintenance disputes or litigations?
  • How many units are currently owner-occupied vs rented?
  • Is there a competing management company operating nearby?
  • Can I outsource the caretaking duties?
