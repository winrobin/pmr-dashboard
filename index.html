Guide: How to Buy PMR指南：如何购买PMR
▶1. What is PMR?1. 什么是PMR?

Property Management Rights (PMR) is a uniquely Australian business model — most common in Queensland. You buy the right to manage a strata complex — not the building itself.

In this model:

  • Caretaking" — you maintain common areas: gardens, pools, BBQ, clean, minor repairs
  • Letting租赁管理: you manage short-term or long-term rentals for unit owners — earn commission on each booking or lease
  • You are paid by the Body Corporate (for caretaking) AND by unit owners (for letting)

Three Types

TypeWhat You BuyPrice
Business Only纯商业Caretaking + Letting agreement only$500K-$1.5M
MR + UnitManagement rights + manager's apartment$1M-$3M+
MR + FreeholdManagement rights + own a unit (you own the property too)$2M-$5M+

Permanent vs Holiday vs Mixed

  • Permanent (长租): 6-12 month leases, stable income, lower returns, less work
  • Holiday (短租): Airbnb-style, higher returns (~2-3x permanent), more work, seasonal
  • Mixed (混合): Both types, balance of stable income + higher returns, most common
▶2. Step-by-Step Process2. 购买流程

Phase 1: Preparation (Weeks 1-4)

1Research & Self-Assessment调研与自我评估

  • Define your budget: How much can you afford? 30-40% deposit minimum for most lenders
  • Decide: Do you want to live on-site (MR+Unit) or just run the business?
  • Consider: Holiday vs Permanent — holiday earns more but requires more work/time; permanent is more passive
  • Skill check: communication, organisation, basic maintenance/handyman skills are very helpful

2Assemble Your Professional Team组建专业团队

Four professionals you MUST engage:

  • Management Rights Broker — Specialised broker who finds listings, negotiates, guides you through process. Most brokers charge 3-5% commission paid by seller, not you.
  • Management Rights Financier — Specialist lender who understands PMR loans. Most major banks (CBA, NAB, ANZ) + specialist lenders (MRM, Fundd). Expect 30-40% deposit.
  • Management Rights Accountant — NOT a regular accountant! They verify the seller's profit figures, advise on tax/structure (sole trader, company, trust?), assess cash flow. This is critical — they are your safety net.
  • Management Rights Lawyer/Solicitor — Reviews care-taking agreements, letting agreements, Body Corporate by-laws and minutes, manages BC approval process.
💡 Tip: Ask each professional specifically about their management rights experience. A regular accountant or lawyer who hasn't done PMR deals before can miss critical issues.

3Get Pre-Approved for Finance获得贷款预批准

  • Most lenders require 30-40% deposit (some may go as low as 20% if you have strong serviceable income)
  • Lenders will assess if the business's net income can service the loan
  • Pre-approval gives you buying power and credibility with sellers
  • Factor in additional costs: stamp duty (~3-4%), legal fees ($3-5K), accountant fees ($3-5K), broker fees (covered by seller for most)

Phase 2: Finding & Evaluating (Weeks 5-12)

4Search & Shortlist搜索与筛选

  • Your broker will present opportunities matching your criteria
  • Key websites: AccomProperties.com.au, ResortBrokers.com.au, TheOnsiteManager.com.au
  • Focus on: BC% ratio (60-70% ideal), multiplier (3-5x ideal), contract length (10+ yrs), letting pool size and composition
  • Attend inspections — look at the complex, the manager's unit, common areas, relationship with current owners/tenants

5Make an Offer出价

  • Submit your offer through your broker to the seller's agent
  • Once accepted, contracts (residential + business) are prepared
  • Contracts include conditions: pest/building inspections, accounting due diligence, finance approval, Body Corporate approval

6Due Diligence (CRITICAL — Don't Skip This!)尽职调查（关键！）

This is the most important phase. Your professional team does this:

  • Accounting: Your accountant independently verifies all income — BC salary, letting commissions, pool fees, cleaning income. They check trust account records, occupancy rates, historical financial statements, and cash flow projections
  • Legal: Your lawyer reviews caretaking agreement (what you must do, what you're paid, term length, renewal options, CPI increases), letting agreement (commission rates, terms), Body Corporate by-laws, past minutes (look for disputes, special levies, upcoming major expenses)
  • Physical: Building/pest inspections on manager unit + common areas. Check pool condition, gym equipment, common area wear
  • Letting Pool Analysis: How many units currently in your pool? Composition (permanent vs holiday)? Occupancy rate? Growth potential? Risk of losing units? How many are owner-occupied, how many are let by you, how many use outside agents?
  • Body Corporate Relationship: Review recent minutes. Are there personality clashes, disputes, unresolved issues? A good relationship with the BC committee is essential for long-term success
🚨 WARNING: Many first-time buyers skip adequate due diligence. Never buy based solely on the seller's financial statements — always have your own specialist PMR accountant verify everything.

Phase 3: Approval & Settlement (Weeks 12-20)

7Final Finance Approval最终贷款批准

  • After successful due diligence, your financier provides final approval based on accountant's profit verification

8Body Corporate Approval业主大会批准

  • The Body Corporate must approve the assignment of management rights to you. Your lawyer manages this. The BC will interview you — they want to like you and trust you.

9Licensing执照

  • You need a Resident Letting Agent (RLA) licence in QLD to manage rentals if you live on-site
  • This can often be obtained during the settlement period — start early
  • Caretaking duties do NOT require a separate licence
  • Your lawyer/broker can guide you on specific requirements

10Settlement交割

  • Transfer of management rights and manager unit ownership
  • You receive the keys, contracts, letting agreements, and trust account
  • Introduce yourself to Body Corporate committee and unit owners
  • Start managing the business!
▶3. Due Diligence Checklist3. 尽职调查清单

Financial

  • Verify net income independently (don't trust seller's figures)
  • Check all sources of income: BC salary, all letting commissions, pool fees, cleaning income, other revenue
  • Review 3+ years financial statements
  • Check trust account records — any discrepancies?
  • Review current occupancy rates (permanent: 95%+ expected; holiday: varies 60-70% average)
  • Calculate actual net profit after all operating expenses
  • Confirm CPI increase clauses in contracts

Legal

  • Review caretaking agreement: duties, responsibilities, remuneration, term, renewal options, CPI increases
  • Review letting agreement: commission rates, terms, obligations
  • Review Body Corporate by-laws
  • Review past 2 years Body Corporate meeting minutes — any disputes, special levies, maintenance issues?
  • Confirm all legal obligations are current and in order
  • Check for any outstanding disputes or legal claims

Physical

  • Building inspection on manager's unit
  • Pest inspection
  • Inspect common areas: pool condition, gym, BBQ, gardens, lifts
  • Check if any major capital expenditure is upcoming (special levies?)
  • Assess maintenance backlog — how much work is behind?

Letting Pool

  • Count actual units currently in your pool
  • Composition: permanent vs holiday lets?
  • Occupancy rate average over 12 months
  • Growth potential: Can you add more units? How many outside agent units could you convert?
  • Risk of loss: Any owners considering leaving the pool? Using outside agents?

Body Corporate Relationship

  • Meet the BC committee — do you like them? Do they like you?
  • Review relationship history — are there ongoing disputes?
  • Confirm the BC supports the manager and the business
  • Check if the caretaking remuneration is competitive
▶4. Common Pitfalls (READ CAREFULLY)4. 常见陷阱（仔细阅读）
🚨 Pitfall 1: Inadequate Due Diligence The #1 reason management rights purchases fail. Never rely on seller's figures alone — always have a specialist PMR accountant independently verify every number. Many buyers skip this to save $3-5K and lose hundreds of thousands.
🚨 Pitfall 2: Poor Body Corporate Relationship Your entire income depends on the BC committee. They can make your life miserable or beautiful. A deteriorating relationship = stress, reduced income, and potentially losing the contract. Review past BC minutes carefully — look for disputes, personality clashes, unresolved issues.
🚨 Pitfall 3: Ambiguous or Outdated Agreements Poorly written caretaking agreements can mean more work for the same pay. Ensure your duties and remuneration are clearly defined. Check: Can the BC increase your workload without increasing your pay? Are renewal terms clearly defined?
🚨 Pitfall 4: Loss of Letting Pool Units While usually stable, units can leave the pool if owners decide to: live in the unit themselves, use outside letting agents, leave them untenanted, or sell to someone who doesn't want a letting manager. Check how stable the current letting pool is and what risk there is of losing units.
🚨 Pitfall 5: Underestimating Workload Many buyers underestimate the daily workload and responsibilities. Day-to-day operations include: cleaning, minor maintenance, garden maintenance, tenant management, emergency calls. Be realistic about how many hours per week this takes.
🚨 Pitfall 6: Insufficient Capital Ensure you have enough for: purchase price + deposit + stamp duty (~3-4%) + legal fees ($3-5K) + accountant fees ($3-5K) + broker fees (usually covered by seller) + buffer for unexpected expenses. Don't run out of cash in the first few months.
🚨 Pitfall 7: Changes in Market Conditions Holiday letting is particularly sensitive to tourism fluctuations, economic downturns, and unexpected events (like pandemics). Don't rely too heavily on peak season income — plan for the worst case scenario.
🚨 Pitfall 8: Choosing the Wrong Advisors Using a regular accountant or lawyer who has never done PMR deals is a major risk. Ensure all your advisors have specific experience in management rights. They will catch issues that a generalist would miss.
▶5. Tips & Online Resources5. 心得与在线资源

Key Tips From Industry Experts

  • The "30/40 Rule": You need at least 30-40% deposit for most PMR loans. Some specialist lenders may go lower, but expect higher interest rates
  • The "Multiplier Rule": A good PMR has a multiplier of 3-5x. Above 6x starts to look overpriced
  • The "BC Ratio Rule": BC salary as % of net income should be 50-70%. Above 80% = caretaker trap (your income is mostly fixed, no real growth)
  • The "Pool Stability Check" Before buying, ask: How many units are currently in the pool? How long have they been in the pool? Are any considering leaving?
  • The "Body Corporate Test" If you don't like and trust the BC committee, DON'T BUY. A bad BC relationship can destroy your business
  • The "Workload Estimate" Permanent letting: ~15-20 hrs/week. Holiday letting: ~25-40 hrs/week. Holiday earns more but requires significantly more time

Important Things to Ask the Seller

  • What is the current Body Corporate relationship like?
  • How many units are currently in the letting pool, and how long have they been there?
  • Are any units likely to leave the pool after the sale?
  • What major maintenance or capital expenditure is upcoming?
  • How many hours per week does the business take?
  • Are there any disputes or issues with unit owners?
  • Has the BC ever threatened to reduce the manager's remuneration?
  • What percentage of income is from caretaking vs letting?
  • Is the manager's unit included in the sale price?
💡 Pro Tip: Talk to CURRENT or FORMER management rights owners. They will give you the real picture — not the broker's polished version. Ask about the BC relationship, the workload, the money, the stress, the good and the bad.
