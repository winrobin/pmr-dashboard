▶1. What is PMR?

PMR (Property Management Rights) is a uniquely Australian business model, most common in Queensland. It combines two roles:

  • Caretaking — maintaining common areas (gardens, pools, BBQ areas, laundries)
  • Letting — managing short-term or long-term rentals for unit owners who don't want to self-manage

How It Works

The Body Corporate (BC) — the strata committee that owns the complex — appoints a manager via a Management Rights Agreement. The manager receives:

  • BC Salary (Remuneration) — fixed annual payment from the BC for caretaking duties
  • Letting Commission — percentage of rental income for each unit they manage
💡 You buy the agreement (the right to run the business), not the property itself.

Three Types

TypeWhat You BuyTypical Price
Business OnlyCaretaking + Letting agreement$500K - $1.5M
MR + Unit (MLU)PMR biz + manager's apartment$1M - $3M+
MR + FreeholdPMR biz + own a unit$2M - $5M+

Permanent vs Holiday

  • Permanent — long-term rentals (6-12 months), stable income, lower returns
  • Holiday — short-term bookings (Airbnb-style), higher returns, more work
  • Mixed — both (most versatile but most complex)

▶3. How to Evaluate a PMR Deal

① BC Salary % — THE Most Important Metric

BC% = BC Salary / Net Income × 100
BC%MeaningAction
50-70%Healthy balance✅ Good
70-80%Leaning caretaker⚠️ Caution
80%+Caretaker trap❌ Avoid
🚨 BC >80% means no real letting business. Your income is fixed salary only — no growth.

② Multiplier

Multiplier = Asking Price / Annual Net Profit
MultiplierAssessment
3-5xExcellent value (recoup in 3-5 years)
5-6xFair market
6x+Overpriced

③ Commission Per Unit Per Week

$/wk = (Net Income - BC Salary) / Pool Units / 52
$/weekMeaning
$60-85Normal Brisbane range
$40-60Below average
<$40Abnormally low — investigate!
🚨 Under $40/week = letting business may be unprofitable or BC salary inflated.

④ Letting Pool Ratio

Pool Ratio = Pool Units / Total Units × 100
✅ Higher ratio = more letting income. Ideal: 30-60%
🚨 Below 15% = minimal letting; Above 70% = high workload

⑤ Contract Years Remaining

YearsMeaning
15+Excellent security
10-15Good — verify top-up history
<10Risk — needs top-up strategy

⑥ Operational Burden

  • On-site required? (Must you live there?)
  • Fixed office hours? (vs flexible contactable)
  • Facilities: Pool? Gym? BBQ? Lift? (More = more work)
  • Can you sub-contract cleaning/gardening? (Ideal)
▶4. Financial Analysis

Return on Investment

ROI = (Net Income / Asking Price) × 100
ROIAssessment
15-25%Strong return
10-15%Market average
<10%Weak — reconsider

Leveraged Return (With Mortgage)

Cash Flow = Net Income - Annual Mortgage

Example: $800K deal, 70% LVR loan @ 6% = ~$33K/yr mortgage

$128K Net Income - $33K Mortgage = $95K cash flow
💡 BC salary increases by CPI only. Letting income GROWS with occupancy, rates, and pool expansion.

Typical Operating Costs

  • Cleaning/maintenance: $5K-$15K/yr
  • Marketing/advertising: $3K-$8K/yr
  • Insurance: $2K-$5K/yr
  • Software/accounting: $2K-$4K/yr
  • Staff (if employed): $40K-$80K/yr
▶5. Legal & Regulatory

Governing Law

Queensland: Body Corporate and Community Management Act 1997

Sets rules for agreement structure, manager rights, top-ups, disputes.

License Requirements

  • Caretaking only — No license needed
  • Letting + caretaking — Resident Letting Agent (RLA) license
  • Property management — Full real estate license

Due Diligence Checklist

  • ☐ Full management agreement (all terms)
  • ☐ 3 years financial statements (audited)
  • ☐ Body Corporate meeting minutes (last 3 years)
  • ☐ Current letting pool schedule
  • ☐ Top-up history and next due date
  • ☐ Maintenance backlog report
  • ☐ Insurance certificate
  • ☐ Competing management rights nearby
  • ☐ Occupancy rates and trends

Gold Coast Market

▶8. Red Flags
🚨 BC Salary >80% — Caretaker trap, no real letting business
🚨 Contract <8 years — Top-up may fail
🚨 Hostile Body Corporate — Check meeting minutes
🚨 Maintenance backlog — Your workload doubles
🚨 Owner-occupier majority >60% — They oppose short-term lets
🚨 Competing PMR next door — Can steal your pool
✅ History of successful top-ups = supportive BC
✅ High occupancy rate = strong demand
✅ Can outsource caretaking = lower workload
