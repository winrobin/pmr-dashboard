PMR Investment Guide

Ch 1 — What is PMR?

Property Management Rights (PMR) give you the right to manage a residential complex — collecting rent, maintaining common areas, and providing caretaker services. You earn salary/caretaking income plus commissions on letting pool units.

Ch 2 — Key Metrics

  • Net Income (NI) — Annual revenue after direct costs. Target >$120K.
  • BC% (Salary/Net) — Body corporate salary as % of net income. Ideal <70%.
  • Multiplier — Purchase price ÷ net income. Lower = better. Target ≤6.5x.
  • Pool Units — Letting pool size. More units = more commission income.
  • Contract Years — Years remaining on management agreement. Target ≥15yr.
  • Weekly Rent/Unit — Average rent per letting pool unit. $60–85/wk typical QLD.

Ch 3 — Scoring

  • BC% → Green ≤70%, Yellow 70–80%, Red >80%
  • Multiplier → Green ≤6.5x, Yellow 6.5–7.5x, Red >7.5x
  • Contract → Green ≥15yr, Yellow 10–14yr, Red <10yr

Ch 4 — Price Tiers

  • T1 <$1M — Entry level, lower NI, good for first-timers
  • T2 $1–1.5M — Mid-range, best ROI balance zone
  • T3 $1.5M+ — Premium, higher NI, more capital required

Ch 5 — Due Diligence Checklist

  • Request last 3 years financials from vendor
  • Check management agreement length and renewal terms
  • Count actual letting pool units (not total complex units)
  • Verify body corporate salary in agreement
  • Check for exclusive management rights clause
  • Confirm caretaker duties are manageable solo or with staff

Ch 6 — Financing PMR

Banks treat PMR as commercial lending. Typical LVR 50–65%. Some lenders specialise in PMR (Bankwest, NAB, BOQ). Separate the real estate component (manager's unit) from the business rights for cleaner financing.

Ch 7 — Understanding the Contract

The caretaking agreement and letting appointment are two separate contracts. The caretaking agreement is registered on title. The letting appointment is between you and each lot owner. Review both with a PMR-specialist solicitor.

Ch 8 — Improving NI After Purchase

  • Grow the letting pool by winning over owner-managed units
  • Review maintenance markup margins
  • Add value-adds: pool cleaning, garden, valet parking contracts
  • Reduce vacancy by active marketing and competitive pricing

Ch 9 — Real Operator Tips

  • Live on-site — your proximity is your competitive advantage
  • Build relationships with your BC committee early
  • Use property management software (Console, Manage My Property) from day 1
  • Hire a relief manager for holidays — factor this into projections
  • Join ARAMA for support and advocacy
  • Watch for "on-site manager burnout" — drives below-market sales you can target

Ch 10 — Licences & Document Checklist

  • Real Estate Licence (QLD) — required to operate a letting business
  • Trust account registration with QCAT
  • ABN + GST registration
  • Public liability insurance (min $10M recommended)
  • Documents to request: management agreement, caretaking agreement, BC financials, last 3yr P&L, rental rolls, letting pool list, BC meeting minutes (last 2 years)
